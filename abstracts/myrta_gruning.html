Myrta Grüning

(Queen's University Belfast)




Nonlinear Optics in Advanced Materials from First Principles

In the past decades, many-body approaches based on the GW approximation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation have become state-of-the-art for calculating optical absorption in solids and nanostructures. In this talk, I will present a real-time approach derived from the non-equilibrium Green's function, that allows extending the GW+BSE approach beyond the linear regime [1,2]. Using this approach, I will address the importance of many-body effects and in particular of excitonic effects for nonlinear optical properties [3]. For example, I will look at the case of single-layer monochalcogenide whose strong Second Harmonic Generation cannot be reproduced within the independent-particle approximation [4]. I will then showcase a few applications to e.g. sum of frequency generation in 2D materials. In the second part of the talk, I will address the computational cost of these approaches and so discuss the possibility of a real-time approach based on time-dependent density-functional theory, that can describe excitonic effects [5] or of alternatives to the real-time approach [6].
