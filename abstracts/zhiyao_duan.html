Zhiyao Duan

(Northwestern Polytechnical University)




Decoding Atomic Structures with Computational X-ray Absorption Spectrum

Renewable energy production and conversion rely on efficient catalysts. Research works trying to gain fundamental understandings on the structure-property relationships of catalysts are increasingly important for developing novel catalysts. EXAFS spectroscopy is a powerful method to probe the atomic structure of catalysts, but the data analysis is prone to artifacts, and the interpretation of obtained results is often problematic. Some of the challenges associated with the conventional approaches for EXAFS analysis are the result of complex, asymmetric bond-length distributions and an overlap of contributions from different coordination shells and multiple-scattering effects.
Often the experimental data alone do not contain sufficient information to recover the 3D structure model of a catalyst unambiguously. In this case, input from first-principles simulations of the structure and dynamics of catalysts using the DFT approach, and/or an empirical model of interatomic interactions, can assist in the interpretation of experimental data, while structure-sensitive information, encoded in EXAFS, can be used to validate theoretical structure models. Herein, we demonstrate how simulated EXAFS spectra using first-principles method could facilitate the structural characterization of various catalysts. And, with the help of more accurate structural assignments, the structure-property relationships of catalysts could be better understood.
