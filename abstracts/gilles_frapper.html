Gilles Frapper

(University of Poitiers)




Emergence of Polynitrogen Networks in M-N Phases under Pressure: En Route to High-Energy Density Materials

Dinitrogen (N2) is the most abundant element on Earth and is mostly unreactive at atmospheric pressure and room temperature. This is mainly due to its strong triple bond. Hypothetical molecules containing nitrogen atoms connected by unstable single or double bonds have been proposed to decompose to very stable N2 molecules with the release of large amounts of energy. Consequently, the quest for materials with a high nitrogen content is still regarded as a "holy grail" in science, due to their chemical interest and potential as environmentally friendly high-energy density materials (HEDMs). Unfortunately, neutral polynitrogen compounds are expected to have a small energy barrier toward decomposition, such as the long-sought but still unobtained tetrahedrane N4. To overcome these kinetic and thermodynamic issues, we and others proposed that mixing an electropositive element M or molecular ionic species with nitrogen may lead to stable solid-state MxNy compounds where the polynitrogen anionic (N)yq- nets would be stabilized by a sea of cations. An alternative approach to the synthesis of "polymerised nitrogen" is the modulation of pressure, which can overcome the high kinetic barriers encountered in such processes and thermodynamically stabilise the MxNy products, as illustrated in Figure 1. The resulting high-pressure compound can then be recovered to ambient conditions.
This talk will present a brief overview of the evolutionary (genetic) algorithm, with a particular focus on its contribution to the field of crystal structure prediction [1,2]. Here, the global optimiser is combined with a first-principles local optimiser, creating a powerful tool for scanning the energy landscape. The DFT calculations are performed at the PBE, SCAN or HSE06 levels (+vdW corrections), depending on the property being evaluated. These include crystal structure, energy, phonons, AIMD, energy gap, bands, DOS, COHP, COBI, and so forth.

Figure 1. Conceptual design of polynitrogen-based materials.

Molecular units such as N2q- dumbbells, cyclo-N5- pentazolate, 6-ring N6q-, polymeric infinite chains, and 2D/3D polynitrogen nets have been identified on binary polynitrogen-based compounds MxNy (M=Mg [3], Ba [4], Sn [5] Xe [6], transition metals [7], Pb…) that are dynamically and thermodynamically stable on their respective potential energy surfaces at a given pressure. Finally, the electronic and detonation properties of the in silico predicted metastable compounds but dynamically and thermally stable at ambient conditions will be presented. Will our results lead to attempts at synthesis? That is one of the aims of our work in the field of 'Computational Materials Discovery', supported by GENCI (IDRIS, TGCC), ANR, Feder Nouvelle Aquitaine, CNRS, the EUR Intree and UPsquared programs of Poitiers University
