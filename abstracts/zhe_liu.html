Machine Learning Tools with Domain Knowledge to Accelerate the Development of Perovskite Solar Cells

The carbon-neutral goal puts a time-sensitive requirement for the R&D of renewable energy materials, that is to rapidly develop high-performance, low-cost new materials and achieve industrial application in a relative short timeframe. For solar PV, the global deployment needs to increase nearly 70-fold next 30 years. The pressure is imminent for accelerating the R&D of new PV materials. Recently, machine learning is expected to accelerate the research of emerging PV technology, such as, perovskite solar cells. This work focuses on machine learning tools that incorporate domain knowledge for perovskite solar cells.
As an example, we discuss three important R&D tasks: autonomous optimization of fabrication process, high-throughput analysis of spectral characterization data, and intelligent screening of surface passivation materials. Firstly, regarding the high-dimensional process optimization, Bayesian optimization can quickly establish the process-performance correlation. We achieve an optimization of six process parameters simultaneously and find the optimal process condition for spray coating of perovskite thin films. Secondly, for the spectral characterization of perovskite thin films, machine learning has a common challenge of data scarcity in experiments. Transfer learning could learn domain physics based on theoretical simulations. After training with simulated data, only a small amount of experimental data is needed in the secondary training to achieve high prediction accuracy. We demonstrate this concept from the reflection/transmission spectra, and extract the thin film thickness accurately. Finally, we tackle the materials screening challenges of surface passivation materials for perovskites. By converting a molecule structure to a set of molecular descriptors, machine learning regression model can establish the relationship between the molecular characteristics and device performance. We are then able to identify a few high-quality organic molecules to achieve higher device efficiency. In short, these proposed applications combine machine learning methods with domain knowledge and experience related to photovoltaic materials. To conclude, we will have an open discussion on the perspective of the generative AI tools (e.g., GPT) for materials science research.
